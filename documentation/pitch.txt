Project Name: BRACE
Biomechanical Real-time Adaptive Clinical Evaluation

---

BRACE - 90 Second Pitch

HOOK:
Every year, 200,000 ACL tears happen in the US alone. Most are preventable
-- the warning signs are visible in how you move, but you need a biomechanics
lab to detect them. We put that lab in your browser.

DEMO:
Open laptop, point webcam at someone doing squats. Show the skeleton overlay
appearing instantly. Point to the injury risk panel: "See that -- knee valgus
at 22 degrees, flagged as medium ACL risk. That's the same measurement a
sports medicine clinic charges $300 for."

HOW IT WORKS:
We run YOLO11 pose estimation on a GPU, normalize the skeleton into a
body-relative coordinate frame so we can compare anyone's movement to anyone
else's, then run 12 biomechanical metrics every frame -- angular velocities,
center of mass tracking, isolation forest anomaly detection -- all streaming
at 30fps.

DIFFERENTIATOR:
Existing tools are either offline batch processors or simple angle calculators.
We do real-time fatigue detection using spectral median frequency -- a technique
borrowed from clinical EMG research. We detect when your form is degrading
before you feel it.

PRIMARY FOCUS - INDIVIDUAL HEALTH:
This is a personal health tool first. Think total body workouts at home or
in the gym. You tell BRACE about your body -- "I tore my ACL two years ago,
my right shoulder clicks when I press overhead" -- through a conversational
Gemini agent that builds your injury profile. Then every time you work out,
BRACE knows YOUR vulnerabilities. The squat threshold that's fine for someone
else triggers a warning for you 35% earlier. Your shoulder gets extra
monitoring during overhead presses. Movement-specific guidelines adapt to
your history -- not generic advice, but "keep knees tracking over toes,
IMPORTANT given your ACL history."

REAL EXAMPLE - "IS THIS WORKOUT SAFE FOR ME?":
You rolled your ankle forward in a rock climbing fall. It still bothers you
months later. You find a 20-minute HIIT video on YouTube and want to know:
is this safe for me before I commit? Drop the video URL into BRACE. It
analyzes every movement in the workout -- the box jumps, the lateral lunges,
the single-leg hops -- against YOUR ankle injury profile. Before you even
start, BRACE flags: "Minutes 4-6 contain plyometric landings with high
ankle dorsiflexion load. Given your anterior ankle injury, consider the
low-impact modification or skip this segment." You get a risk-annotated
timeline of someone else's workout, personalized to your body, before you
do a single rep.

Upload your own workout video after the fact? BRACE scrubs it for the
highest-risk moments and marks them on the timeline so you can review
exactly where your form broke down and why.

MULTI-PERSON / TEAM USE CASE:
BRACE tracks every person in the frame simultaneously -- each with their own
skeleton, their own fatigue curve, their own injury risk profile. Point a
camera at a full practice field and the system independently monitors every
player's biomechanics in parallel.

For coaches: see who's accumulating fatigue fastest, whose form is degrading,
who's compensating on one side. Make substitution decisions backed by data
instead of guessing who looks tired. Catch the player whose knee valgus is
creeping up before they plant wrong and tear something.

The same cross-cut re-identification system that tracks players through camera
angle changes means you can switch between field cameras and BRACE keeps each
player's identity and fatigue history intact.

But the core pitch stays health-first: we help individuals protect their
bodies during exercise, and the multi-person capability means that scales
from one person in a living room to an entire team on a field.
